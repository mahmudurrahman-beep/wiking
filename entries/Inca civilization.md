## Origins
- Emerged in the **Andes Mountains** around the early 15th century.  
- Founded by **Pachacuti Inca Yupanqui** in 1438, who expanded Cusco into the capital of a vast empire.  
- Known as **Tawantinsuyu** (“Realm of the Four Parts”).  

## Geography
- Stretched from **Quito (Ecuador)** in the north to **Santiago (Chile)** in the south.  
- Covered ~**2 million km²**, with a population of ~**12 million** at its peak[43dcd9a7-70db-4a1f-b0ae-981daa162054](https://www.worldhistory.org/Inca_Civilization/?citationMarker=43dcd9a7-70db-4a1f-b0ae-981daa162054&citationId=1&citationId=2 "Wikipedia").  

## Society & Culture
- Government: **Divine monarchy**, ruled by the **Sapa Inca**.  
- Language: **Quechua** (official), with many regional languages.  
- Religion: Polytheistic, worshipped **Inti (Sun God)**, practiced rituals and sacrifices.  
- Economy: Based on **agriculture (maize, potatoes, quinoa)**, supported by terracing and irrigation.  
- Social system: **Ayllu** (community groups) organized labor and resources.  

## Achievements
- **Engineering**: Built extensive **road networks (over 40,000 km)** across mountains and valleys.  
- **Architecture**: Mastery of stonework without mortar; sites like **Machu Picchu**, **Sacsayhuamán**, and **Ollantaytambo**.  
- **Agriculture**: Terraced farming, advanced irrigation, and storage systems.  
- **Administration**: Used **quipu** (knotted strings) for record‑keeping.  

## Decline
- Civil war between brothers **Huascar and Atahualpa** weakened the empire (1529–1532).  
- Spanish conquest led by **Francisco Pizarro** in **1533** captured Atahualpa.  
- Final resistance ended in **1572** with the fall of Vilcabamba[43dcd9a7-70db-4a1f-b0ae-981daa162054](https://www.britannica.com/topic/Inca?citationMarker=43dcd9a7-70db-4a1f-b0ae-981daa162054&citationId=1&citationId=3 "Wikipedia").  

## Legacy
- Inca descendants still live in the Andes, preserving **Quechua language and traditions**.  
- Their innovations in **architecture, agriculture, and governance** continue to inspire modern science and engineering.  
- **Machu Picchu** remains one of the most iconic archaeological sites in the world.
