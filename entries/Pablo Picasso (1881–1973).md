## Definition
**Pablo Picasso** was a Spanish painter, sculptor, printmaker, ceramicist, and stage designer. He is regarded as one of the most influential artists of the 20th century and a co‑founder of the **Cubist movement**.

## Early Life
- Born: **October 25, 1881**, Málaga, Spain.  
- Showed extraordinary artistic talent from childhood.  
- Studied at art schools in Barcelona and Madrid.  

## Artistic Periods
- **Blue Period (1901–1904)** – Melancholic works dominated by shades of blue, reflecting poverty and despair.  
- **Rose Period (1904–1906)** – Warmer tones, featuring circus performers and themes of joy.  
- **African Art & Primitivism (1907–1909)** – Inspired by Iberian sculpture and African masks.  
- **Cubism (1909–1919)** – Co‑founded with Georges Braque; broke objects into geometric forms (*Les Demoiselles d’Avignon*, 1907).  
- **Neoclassicism & Surrealism (1920s–1930s)** – Experimented with classical forms and surrealist imagery.  

## Masterpieces
- *Les Demoiselles d’Avignon* (1907) – Landmark in modern art.  
- *Guernica* (1937) – Powerful anti‑war mural depicting the bombing of Guernica during the Spanish Civil War.  
- *The Weeping Woman* (1937).  
- *Girl before a Mirror* (1932).  

## Legacy
- Produced an estimated **50,000 artworks** across painting, sculpture, ceramics, and prints.  
- Revolutionized modern art with Cubism and constant reinvention of style.  
- Died: **April 8, 1973**, Mougins, France.  
- Remembered as a genius who reshaped the boundaries of artistic expression.  

---

*Picasso’s restless creativity and bold experimentation made him a defining figure of modern art, whose influence continues worldwide.*
