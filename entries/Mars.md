## Introduction
Mars is the fourth planet from the Sun and often called the **Red Planet** due to its iron oxide-rich soil. It is a terrestrial planet with a thin atmosphere and surface features reminiscent of both Earth and the Moon.

## History / Origins
- Observed since antiquity by Babylonian, Egyptian, and Greek astronomers.  
- Named after **Mars**, the Roman god of war.  
- Modern exploration began with telescopes in the 17th century, later expanded by spacecraft missions.

- Diameter: ~6,779 km (about half of Earthâ€™s).  
- Atmosphere: Thin, mostly carbon dioxide.  
- Surface: Volcanoes (Olympus Mons, the tallest in the Solar System), valleys, deserts, and polar ice caps.  
- Moons: **Phobos** and **Deimos**, small irregular satellites.  
- Day length: ~24.6 hours, similar to Earth.  
- Year length: ~687 Earth days.  

## Legacy / Impact
- Central to human imagination as a possible home for extraterrestrial life.  
- Target of numerous missions: Viking (1970s), Mars Rovers (Spirit, Opportunity, Curiosity, Perseverance), and orbiters.  
- Future plans include human exploration and potential colonization.
