## Definition
The **Bronze Age** is defined by the widespread use of **bronze**, an alloy of copper and tin (sometimes arsenic), for tools, weapons, and art. It represents a key stage in human technological and cultural development.

## Timeline (Regional Variations)
- **Near East & South Asia**: ~3300–1200 BCE  
- **East Asia (China)**: ~3100–300 BCE  
- **Europe**: ~3200–900 BCE  
- **Britain**: ~1900 BCE onwards  
- **Africa**: varied, often later adoption  

## Key Features
- **Metallurgy**: Bronze replaced stone for tools and weapons.  
- **Urbanization**: Rise of cities and complex societies (Mesopotamia, Egypt, Indus Valley, China).  
- **Trade**: Long-distance exchange of tin, copper, and luxury goods.  
- **Writing Systems**: First scripts emerged (cuneiform in Mesopotamia, hieroglyphs in Egypt).  
- **Inventions**: Wheel, plow, sailing ships.  

## Major Civilizations
- **Mesopotamia (Sumer, Akkad, Babylon)**  
- **Ancient Egypt (Old & Middle Kingdoms)**  
- **Indus Valley Civilization**  
- **Shang Dynasty (China)**  
- **Minoan & Mycenaean Greece**  

## Collapse
- Around **1200 BCE**, many Bronze Age civilizations in the Eastern Mediterranean collapsed due to a mix of invasions (Sea Peoples), climate change, and internal strife. This ushered in the **Iron Age**[43dcd9a7-70db-4a1f-b0ae-981daa162054](https://www.history.com/articles/bronze-age?citationMarker=43dcd9a7-70db-4a1f-b0ae-981daa162054&citationId=3 "History").  

## Legacy
- The Bronze Age laid the foundations for **writing, trade networks, urban life, and state-level societies**.  
- It represents humanity’s first step into large-scale metallurgy and complex civilization.
