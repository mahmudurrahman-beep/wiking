## Definition
**Aboriginal Australians** are the Indigenous peoples of the Australian mainland and most of its islands (excluding the Torres Strait Islanders, who are ethnically distinct). They represent diverse nations, languages, and traditions deeply connected to land, sea, and spirituality.

## Population
- **2021 Census**: ~944,000 people identified as Aboriginal (about 3.7% of Australia’s population).  
- Communities are spread across urban, rural, and remote regions.  

## Languages
- Historically, several hundred Aboriginal languages were spoken.  
- Many are now endangered or extinct due to colonization.  
- Today, Aboriginal English and Kriol are widely used alongside Australian English.  

## Religion and Beliefs
- **Traditional Spirituality** – Rooted in the *Dreamtime* (or *Dreaming*), which explains creation, ancestral beings, and moral laws.  
- **Christianity** – Adopted by many communities during and after colonization.  
- **Secular/Other Beliefs** – Increasingly common in modern times.  

## Culture
- Rich traditions in **art, music, dance, and storytelling**.  
- **Dot painting** and **rock art** are iconic artistic forms.  
- Oral traditions preserve history, law, and cosmology.  
- Strong emphasis on kinship, community, and custodianship of land.  

## History
- Aboriginal peoples lived in Australia for tens of thousands of years before European arrival.  
- **1788** – British colonization began, leading to dispossession, violence, and population decline.  
- Policies of assimilation and the *Stolen Generations* disrupted families and culture.  
- **2008** – Australian government formally apologized for past mistreatment.  

## Contemporary Issues
- Efforts to preserve languages and cultural heritage.  
- Advocacy for land rights and recognition of sovereignty.  
- Addressing health, education, and economic disparities.  
- Political movements for constitutional recognition and a “Voice to Parliament.”  

## Legacy
- Aboriginal Australians maintain one of the world’s oldest continuous cultures.  
- Their knowledge of land, ecology, and spirituality continues to shape Australia’s identity.  
- The Aboriginal flag (black, red, and yellow) is a symbol of pride and resilience.  

---

*Aboriginal Australians embody resilience and continuity, preserving traditions while navigating modern challenges and asserting their place in Australia’s future.*
