## Definition
The **Association of Southeast Asian Nations (ASEAN)** is a political and economic organization of Southeast Asian countries that promotes cooperation, peace, and regional integration. Its motto is **“One Vision, One Identity, One Community.”**

## Origins
- Founded: **8 August 1967** in Bangkok, Thailand.  
- Founding members: **Indonesia, Malaysia, Philippines, Singapore, Thailand**.  
- Expanded to include: **Brunei (1984), Vietnam (1995), Laos (1997), Myanmar (1997), Cambodia (1999)**.  

## Current Membership
- **10 countries**: Brunei, Cambodia, Indonesia, Laos, Malaysia, Myanmar, Philippines, Singapore, Thailand, Vietnam.  
- Headquarters: **Jakarta, Indonesia**.  
- Anthem: *The ASEAN Way*.  

## Objectives
- Promote **regional peace and stability**.  
- Foster **economic growth, trade, and investment**.  
- Encourage **cultural exchange and cooperation**.  
- Address **regional challenges** like climate change, security, and health.  

## Key Institutions
- **ASEAN Summit** – Annual meeting of leaders.  
- **ASEAN Secretariat** – Administrative body in Jakarta.  
- **ASEAN Free Trade Area (AFTA)** – Economic integration initiative.  
- **ASEAN Regional Forum (ARF)** – Security dialogue platform.  

## Importance
- Represents a population of over **650 million people**.  
- Combined GDP: Over **$3 trillion**.  
- Acts as a bridge between **Asia-Pacific powers** (China, India, US, Japan).  
- Plays a central role in regional diplomacy and trade agreements.  

---

*ASEAN is a cornerstone of Southeast Asian cooperation, balancing economic growth with political stability and cultural unity.*
