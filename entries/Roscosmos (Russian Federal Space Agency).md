## Definition
**Roscosmos**, officially the *State Corporation for Space Activities “Roscosmos”*, is Russia’s national space agency. It oversees space science, exploration, satellite launches, and human spaceflight programs.

## Origins
- Founded: **1992**, after the dissolution of the Soviet Union.  
- Predecessor: Soviet space program, which launched **Sputnik (1957)** and **Yuri Gagarin (1961)**, the first human in space.  
- Became a state corporation in **2015**.  

## Headquarters & Structure
- Headquarters: **Moscow, Russia**.  
- Operates launch facilities at **Baikonur Cosmodrome (Kazakhstan)** and **Vostochny Cosmodrome (Russia)**.  
- Works with Russian aerospace companies and research institutes.  

## Key Achievements
- **Soyuz Program** – reliable spacecraft used for decades in human spaceflight.  
- **Progress Cargo Ships** – resupply missions to the International Space Station (ISS).  
- **ISS Partnership** – major role in building and operating the ISS alongside NASA, ESA, JAXA, and CSA.  
- **Luna Program Revival** – renewed missions to the Moon (e.g., Luna‑25 in 2023).  
- **Mars & Deep Space Missions** – collaborations with ESA and other partners.  

## Current Focus
- Developing the **Russian Orbital Station (ROS)** to replace ISS participation after 2030.  
- Expanding satellite networks for communications, navigation (GLONASS), and Earth observation.  
- Continuing human spaceflight with Soyuz and future spacecraft designs.  

## Legacy
- Roscosmos inherits the prestige of the Soviet space program.  
- Despite funding challenges, it remains a central player in global space exploration.  
- Symbolizes Russia’s commitment to maintaining a strong presence in space.
