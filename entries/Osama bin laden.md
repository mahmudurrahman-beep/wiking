## Early Life
- Born: **10 March 1957**, Riyadh, Saudi Arabia.  
- Son of a wealthy construction magnate; inherited significant resources.  
- Studied economics and management but became radicalized in youth.  

## Rise to Militancy
- Fought in the **Soviet–Afghan War (1979–1989)**, funding and organizing fighters.  
- Founded **al-Qaeda in 1988**, aiming to unite Muslims in global jihad.  
- Expelled from Saudi Arabia in 1991; lived in Sudan, then Afghanistan.  

## Terrorist Activities
- **1998:** Issued fatwa declaring war on the U.S. and its allies.  
- Responsible for:  
  - **1998 U.S. embassy bombings** in Kenya and Tanzania.  
  - **2000 USS Cole bombing** in Yemen.  
  - **September 11, 2001 attacks** on the World Trade Center and Pentagon.  

## Manhunt & Death
- Became the world’s most wanted man after 9/11.  
- Hid in Afghanistan and later Pakistan.  
- **May 2, 2011:** Killed in Abbottabad, Pakistan, during **Operation Neptune Spear** by U.S. Navy SEALs.  

## Legacy
- Symbol of global terrorism and extremism.  
- His death marked a turning point in the “War on Terror.”  
- Al-Qaeda weakened but not eliminated; inspired later extremist groups.
