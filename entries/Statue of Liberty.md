## Introduction
The **Statue of Liberty** is a colossal neoclassical sculpture located on **Liberty Island in New York Harbor, USA**. It was a gift from France to the United States in **1886**, symbolizing freedom, democracy, and friendship between the two nations.

- The internal iron framework was engineered by **Gustave Eiffel**, who later built the Eiffel Tower.  
- Presented to the United States to commemorate its centennial and celebrate shared ideals of liberty.  
- Dedicated on **October 28, 1886**, by President Grover Cleveland.  

## Key Features
- **Height**: 93 meters (305 feet) from ground to torch.  
- **Material**: Copper sheets over an iron framework.  
- **Design**: Depicts **Libertas**, the Roman goddess of liberty, holding a torch and a tablet inscribed with **July 4, 1776** (American Independence Day).  
- **Crown**: Seven rays represent the seven continents and seas.  
- **Location**: Stands at the entrance to New York Harbor, welcoming immigrants and travelers.  

## Legacy / Impact
- Became a universal symbol of freedom and democracy.  
- Designated a **UNESCO World Heritage Site** in 1984.  
- Associated with the immigrant experience, famously referenced in literature and film.  
- Continues to be one of the most visited landmarks in the United States.
