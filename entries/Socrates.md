Socrates (469–399 BCE) was a classical Greek philosopher credited as one of the founders of Western philosophy. He is best known for his method of inquiry and dialogue, later called the "Socratic Method."

## Life and Background
- Born in Athens, Greece.  
- Served as a soldier in the Peloponnesian War.  
- Lived a modest life, focusing on teaching and dialogue.  

## Philosophy
1. **Socratic Method** – Questioning to stimulate critical thinking.  
2. **Ethics** – Belief that virtue is knowledge.  
3. **Critique of Sophists** – Opposed rhetoric without truth.  

## Trial and Death
- Accused of corrupting youth and impiety.  
- Sentenced to death in 399 BCE.  
- Drank hemlock poison as execution.
