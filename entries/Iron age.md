## Definition
The **Iron Age** is a prehistoric and early historic period characterized by the use of **iron metallurgy**. It followed the Bronze Age and introduced stronger, more durable tools and weapons.

## Timeline (Regional Variations)
- **Middle East & Southeastern Europe**: ~1200 BCE  
- **India**: ~1200–600 BCE  
- **China**: ~600 BCE  
- **Northern Europe**: ~500 BCE – 1 CE  
- **Britain**: ~800 BCE – Roman conquest (~43 CE)  

## Key Features
- **Iron Technology**: Iron smelting and forging replaced bronze.  
- **Agriculture**: Iron plows and tools improved farming efficiency.  
- **Warfare**: Iron swords, spears, and armor transformed military power.  
- **Architecture**: Fortified hill forts and settlements expanded.  
- **Culture**: Distinctive art styles (e.g., Celtic La Tène culture in Europe).  

## Major Civilizations
- **Assyrian Empire** (Mesopotamia)  
- **Persian Empire** (Near East)  
- **Celts** (Europe)  
- **Zhou Dynasty** (China)  
- **Maurya Empire** (India)  

## Legacy
- Iron Age societies laid the groundwork for **classical civilizations** (Greek, Roman, Persian).  
- Innovations in metallurgy, agriculture, and warfare reshaped human history.  
- The period ended with the rise of written history and empires in the classical era.
