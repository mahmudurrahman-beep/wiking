## Introduction
The **Milky Way** is the galaxy that contains our Solar System. It is a barred spiral galaxy with hundreds of billions of stars, planets, and nebulae. From Earth, it appears as a faint, milky band of light stretching across the night sky.

## History / Origins
- The name "Milky Way" comes from its appearance as a milky band of light.  
- Ancient civilizations, including the Greeks and Romans, described it in mythology.  
- Modern astronomy revealed it as a vast galaxy, one among billions in the universe.

## Legacy / Impact
- The Milky Way is the most studied galaxy because it is our home.  
- Understanding its structure helps astronomers study galaxy formation and evolution.  
- It is part of the **Local Group**, a cluster of galaxies including Andromeda and Triangulum.
