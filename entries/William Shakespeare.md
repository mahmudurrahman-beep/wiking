## Life
- Born: **23 April 1564**, Stratford-upon-Avon, England.  
- Died: **23 April 1616**, Stratford-upon-Avon.  
- Married **Anne Hathaway** in 1582; had three children (Susanna, Hamnet, Judith).  
- Career: Actor and playwright in London, member of the acting company **Lord Chamberlain’s Men** (later **King’s Men**).  

## Works
- **Comedies:** *A Midsummer Night’s Dream*, *Twelfth Night*, *Much Ado About Nothing*.  
- **Tragedies:** *Hamlet*, *Macbeth*, *Othello*, *King Lear*, *Romeo and Juliet*.  
- **Histories:** *Richard III*, *Henry IV*, *Henry V*.  
- **Poetry:** 154 sonnets, *Venus and Adonis*, *The Rape of Lucrece*.  

## Style & Influence
- Language: Early Modern English, rich in metaphor, imagery, and wordplay.  
- Themes: Love, power, betrayal, fate, ambition, human nature.  
- Innovations: Mastery of blank verse, complex characters, blending comedy and tragedy.  
- Influence: His works shaped English literature, theatre, and even modern storytelling.  

## Legacy
- Known as the **world’s greatest dramatist**.  
- His plays are performed more than any other playwright’s.  
- Inspired countless adaptations in film, theatre, and literature.  
- Famous quote: *“All the world’s a stage, and all the men and women merely players.”* (*As You Like It*).
