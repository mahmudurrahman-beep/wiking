**Nagasaki** is a city in southwestern Japan, located on Kyushu Island. It is historically significant as the second city targeted by a nuclear weapon, when the United States dropped an atomic bomb on **August 9, 1945** during World War II.

## Historical Background
- Developed as a major port city in the 16th century.  
- Known for early contact with Portuguese traders and missionaries.  
- Became Japan’s gateway to the West during the Edo period, especially through the Dutch trading post at Dejima.  

## Atomic Bombing (1945)
- On **August 9, 1945**, the U.S. dropped the bomb *“Fat Man”* on Nagasaki.  
- Immediate destruction: large parts of the city were obliterated.  
- Casualties: estimated **40,000 people killed instantly**, with tens of thousands more dying later from injuries and radiation.  
- The bombing, following Hiroshima, contributed to Japan’s surrender on **August 15, 1945**, ending World War II.  

## Aftermath
- Nagasaki was rebuilt after the war with international support.  
- The **Nagasaki Peace Park** and **Atomic Bomb Museum** commemorate the tragedy and advocate for nuclear disarmament.  
- The **Urakami Cathedral**, destroyed in the blast, was later rebuilt as a symbol of resilience.  

## Modern Nagasaki
- Population: about **400,000** today.  
- Economy: shipbuilding, fishing, tourism, and international trade.  
- Education: home to Nagasaki University and research institutions.  
- Annual **Peace Memorial Ceremony** held on August 9 to honor victims and promote peace.  

---

*Nagasaki stands as both a thriving modern city and a solemn reminder of the devastating impact of nuclear weapons, dedicated to peace and reconciliation.*
