Harvard University, established in **1636**, is the oldest university in the United States and among the most prestigious globally. Its main campus lies along the **Charles River in Cambridge, Massachusetts**, just outside Boston.

## Historical Background

- Founded by the Massachusetts Bay Colony to train clergy.  
- Named after John Harvard, a benefactor who donated his library and half his estate.  
- Evolved from a Puritan college into a modern research university.  

## Academics

- Comprises multiple schools, including:  
  - **Harvard College** (undergraduate)  
  - **Harvard Law School**  
  - **Harvard Medical School**  
  - **Harvard Business School**  
  - **Harvard Kennedy School**  
- Offers undergraduate, graduate, and professional degrees across disciplines.  
- Known for rigorous academics and a strong emphasis on research.  

## Campus and Facilities

- Located in Cambridge, Massachusetts, with additional facilities in Boston.  
- Includes libraries, museums, laboratories, and athletic centers.  
- Harvard Yard is the historic center of the campus.  

## Notable Alumni

- **U.S. Presidents**: John Adams, John F. Kennedy, Barack Obama.  
- **World Leaders**: Ban Ki‑moon, Ellen Johnson Sirleaf.  
- **Innovators**: Bill Gates (attended), Mark Zuckerberg (attended).  
- **Writers and Thinkers**: Ralph Waldo Emerson, T.S. Eliot.  

## Research and Contributions

- Leading research in medicine, genetics, economics, law, and technology.  
- Associated with Nobel Prize winners and groundbreaking discoveries.  
- Houses one of the largest academic libraries in the world.  

## Global Reputation

- Consistently ranked among the top universities worldwide.  
- Member of the Ivy League.  
- Symbol of academic excellence and leadership.  

## Key Facts

- **Founded**: 1636  
- **Location**: Cambridge, Massachusetts, USA  
- **Motto**: *Veritas* (“Truth”)  
- **Colors**: Crimson  
- **Mascot**: John Harvard / Harvard Crimson
