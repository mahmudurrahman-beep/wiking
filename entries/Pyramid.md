## Introduction
A **pyramid** is a monumental structure with a square or triangular base and sloping sides that meet at a point at the top. Pyramids are among the most iconic architectural forms in history, especially associated with ancient Egypt.

## History / Origins
- The earliest pyramids were built in **ancient Egypt** around 2600 BCE.  
- The **Step Pyramid of Djoser** (Saqqara) is considered the first large-scale stone construction.  
- Pyramids were also built in **Mesoamerica** (Maya, Aztec) and other cultures, often serving religious or ceremonial purposes.  

## Key Features
- **Shape**: Symbolized stability and connection between earth and sky.  
- **Function**: Egyptian pyramids served as tombs for pharaohs; Mesoamerican pyramids often functioned as temples.  
- **Construction**: Built with massive stone blocks, requiring advanced engineering and labor organization.
