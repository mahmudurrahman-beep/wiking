## Introduction
The **Taj Mahal** is a white marble mausoleum located in **Agra, India**. It was commissioned in **1632** by the Mughal emperor **Shah Jahan** in memory of his wife **Mumtaz Mahal**. It is widely regarded as one of the most beautiful buildings in the world and a symbol of eternal love.

## History / Origins
- Construction began in 1632 and was completed around 1648, with surrounding structures and gardens finished later.  
- Designed by a team of architects led by **Ustad Ahmad Lahauri**.  
- Built using white marble and precious stones, combining Persian, Islamic, and Indian architectural styles.  

## Key Features
- **Main Dome**: 73 meters high, flanked by four smaller domes.  
- **Minarets**: Four slender towers at each corner of the platform.  
- **Inlay Work**: Precious stones embedded in marble, forming intricate floral patterns.  
- **Gardens**: Charbagh (four-part garden) symbolizing paradise.  
- **Symmetry**: Perfectly balanced design, with the mausoleum at the center.  

## Legacy / Impact
- Recognized as a **UNESCO World Heritage Site** in 1983.  
- Attracts millions of visitors annually, making it one of the most famous tourist destinations in the world.
