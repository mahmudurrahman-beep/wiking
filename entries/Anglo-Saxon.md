## Origins
- Migrated from present-day **Germany, Denmark, and the Netherlands**.  
- Arrived in Britain after the Roman Empire withdrew (~410 CE).  
- Groups included **Angles, Saxons, Jutes, and Frisians**.  

## Timeline
- **410 CE:** Roman withdrawal from Britain.  
- **5th–6th centuries:** Anglo-Saxon kingdoms established (Kent, Wessex, Mercia, Northumbria, East Anglia).  
- **7th century:** Christianisation of Anglo-Saxon England.  
- **9th century:** Viking invasions and Danelaw established.  
- **1066 CE:** Norman Conquest ended Anglo-Saxon rule.  

## Society & Culture
- **Language:** Old English, ancestor of modern English.  
- **Religion:** Initially pagan; later converted to Christianity.  
- **Government:** Local kings and nobles; law codes and charters.  
- **Economy:** Farming, trade, and craftsmanship.  
- **Art & Literature:** Beowulf (epic poem), illuminated manuscripts (Lindisfarne Gospels).  

## Legacy
- Anglo-Saxon law influenced English common law.  
- Old English shaped the modern English language.  
- Place names across England (e.g., “-ham,” “-ton”) trace back to Anglo-Saxon roots.  
- Their culture bridged the gap between Roman Britain and medieval England.
