## Core Principles
- **Social ownership:** Can be public, collective, cooperative, or community-based.  
- **Equality:** Strives to reduce wealth gaps and ensure fair distribution.  
- **Collective welfare:** Prioritizes healthcare, education, and social services.  
- **Economic planning:** Varies from full central planning to mixed economies with regulated markets.  

## Historical Development
- Roots in **19th-century Europe**, influenced by thinkers like **Karl Marx** and **Friedrich Engels**.  
- Early socialist movements sought to address exploitation during the Industrial Revolution.  
- Adopted in different forms:  
  - **Soviet-style socialism:** Centralized, state-controlled.  
  - **Democratic socialism:** Combines democracy with social welfare policies.  
  - **Social democracy:** Market economies with strong welfare states (e.g., Scandinavia).  

## Modern Examples
- **Nordic countries (Sweden, Norway, Denmark):** Social democracy with free markets and strong welfare systems.  
- **Cuba & Vietnam:** State-led socialism with varying degrees of market reforms.  
- **China:** Officially socialist, but operates a hybrid system with capitalist elements.  

## Risks & Trade-offs
- **Pros:** Greater social safety nets, reduced inequality, universal access to services.  
- **Cons:** Risk of inefficiency, reduced innovation, and potential authoritarianism if overly centralized.
