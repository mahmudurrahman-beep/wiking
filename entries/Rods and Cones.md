Photoreceptors are specialized nerve cells in the retina that detect light.  
There are two main types:
- **Rod cells**: Sensitive to low light, provide night vision.  
- **Cone cells**: Sensitive to bright light, provide color vision.

- **Rods** contain *rhodopsin*, a pigment that reacts to low levels of light.  
- **Cones** contain three pigments (photopsins) tuned to different wavelengths:  
  - **S-cones** (short wavelength, blue)  
  - **M-cones** (medium wavelength, green)  
  - **L-cones** (long wavelength, red)
