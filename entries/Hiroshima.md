## Definition
**Hiroshima** is a city in western Japan, located on Honshu Island. It is internationally known as the first city targeted by a nuclear weapon, when the United States dropped an atomic bomb on **August 6, 1945** during World War II.

## Historical Background
- Founded in **1589** as a castle town.  
- Grew into a major military and industrial center by the early 20th century.  
- Population before WWII: about **350,000**.  

## Atomic Bombing (1945)
- On **August 6, 1945**, the U.S. dropped the bomb *“Little Boy”* on Hiroshima.  
- Immediate destruction: most of the city flattened.  
- Casualties: estimated **70,000–80,000 people** killed instantly; tens of thousands more died later from radiation exposure.  
- The bombing marked the beginning of the nuclear age.  

## Aftermath
- Hiroshima was rebuilt after the war, with international support.  
- The **Genbaku Dome (Atomic Bomb Dome)**, part of the Hiroshima Peace Memorial Park, remains preserved as a UNESCO World Heritage Site.  
- The city became a global symbol of peace and nuclear disarmament.  

## Modern Hiroshima
- Population: over **1.1 million** today.  
- Economy: manufacturing, automotive, shipbuilding, and technology.  
- Education: home to Hiroshima University and research institutions.  
- Annual **Peace Memorial Ceremony** held on August 6 to honor victims and promote peace.  

---

*Hiroshima stands as both a thriving modern city and a solemn reminder of the devastating impact of nuclear weapons, dedicated to promoting peace worldwide.*
