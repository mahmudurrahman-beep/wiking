# Cold War (1947–1991)

The **Cold War** was a prolonged geopolitical and ideological struggle between the **United States** (and Western allies) and the **Soviet Union** (and Eastern bloc), marked by rivalry, proxy wars, and nuclear deterrence.

## Causes
- Ideological conflict: **capitalism vs. communism**.  
- Power vacuum after World War II and competition for global influence.

## Major Events
- **Berlin Blockade/Airlift (1948–1949)**.  
- **Korean War (1950–1953)**.  
- **Cuban Missile Crisis (1962)**.  
- **Vietnam War (1955–1975)**.  
- **Space Race** and nuclear arms buildup.  
- **Détente** in the 1970s; renewed tensions in the 1980s; reforms under **Gorbachev**.

## Outcome
- Collapse of the **Soviet Union** and end of communist rule in Eastern Europe (1989–1991).  
- United States emerges as the sole superpower.

## Significance
- Shaped international relations, military strategy, and domestic politics for decades.  
- Spurred technological advances and proxy conflicts around the world.
