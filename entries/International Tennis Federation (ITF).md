## Introduction
The **International Tennis Federation (ITF)** is the governing body of world tennis. It manages international competitions, sets rules, and promotes tennis development globally.

## History / Origins
- Founded on **1 March 1913** in Paris as the **International Lawn Tennis Federation**.  
- Renamed the **International Tennis Federation** in 1977.  
- Headquarters: London, England.  
- Membership: 211 national associations and 6 regional associations.

  - Promotes grassroots tennis development worldwide.  
  - Maintains player rankings across ITF circuits.  
- **Leadership**: Current President is **David Haggerty**.  

## Legacy / Impact
- Expanded tennis into new regions and formats (wheelchair and beach tennis).  
- Provides pathways for young players to progress to ATP and WTA tours.  
- Plays a critical role in maintaining fairness and global standards in tennis.
