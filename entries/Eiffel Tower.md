## Introduction
The **Eiffel Tower** is a wrought-iron lattice tower located on the **Champ de Mars in Paris, France**. It was constructed between **1887 and 1889** as the entrance arch to the **1889 Exposition Universelle (Worldâ€™s Fair)**. Today, it is one of the most recognizable landmarks in the world and a global symbol of France.

## History / Origins
- Designed by French engineer **Gustave Eiffel** and his company.  
- Initially criticized by artists and intellectuals as an eyesore, but later became beloved.  
- Completed in **March 1889**, just in time for the Worldâ€™s Fair celebrating the centennial of the French Revolution.  
- Originally intended to be dismantled after 20 years, but was saved due to its usefulness as a radio transmission tower.  

## Key Features
- **Height**: 330 meters (1,083 feet) after modern antenna additions.  
- **Material**: Iron lattice structure weighing over 10,000 tons.  
- **Levels**: Three public viewing platforms, with restaurants and observation decks.  
- **Lighting**: Illuminated nightly with thousands of sparkling lights.  
- **Visitors**: Attracts more than 7 million visitors annually, making it one of the most visited monuments in the world.  

## Legacy / Impact
- Became a symbol of Paris and French culture.  
- Inspired countless replicas and similar towers worldwide.  
- Serves as a cultural icon featured in films, literature, and art.  
- Continues to function as a broadcast tower in addition to being a tourist attraction.
