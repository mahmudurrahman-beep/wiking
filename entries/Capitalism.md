## Introduction
**Capitalism** is an economic system in which private individuals or businesses own capital goods and control production. Decisions about investment, production, and distribution are guided by competition in free markets, rather than by central planning.

## History / Origins
- Roots trace back to **mercantilism** in the 16th‚Äì18th centuries.  
- Developed strongly during the **Industrial Revolution**, with rapid growth in manufacturing and trade.  
- Formalized by thinkers like **Adam Smith**, who emphasized free markets and limited government intervention.  

## Key Features
- **Private Property**: Individuals and companies own resources and businesses.  
- **Market Economy**: Prices and production are determined by supply and demand.  
- **Profit Motive**: Businesses aim to maximize profits.  
- **Competition**: Encourages innovation and efficiency.  
- **Limited Government Role**: In pure capitalism, government interference is minimal (laissez-faire).  

## Legacy / Impact
- Capitalism has driven global economic growth, technological innovation, and rising living standards.  
- Criticized for creating inequality, exploitation, and environmental challenges.  
- Variants include **free-market capitalism**, **state capitalism**, and **social market economies**.
