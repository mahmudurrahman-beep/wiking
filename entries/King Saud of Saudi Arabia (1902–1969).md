## Early Life
- Born: **January 15, 1902**, in Kuwait City.  
- Son of **King Abdulaziz Ibn Saud**, the founder of modern Saudi Arabia.  
- Played a role in consolidating his father’s kingdom before becoming crown prince in **1933**.  

## Reign (1953–1964)
- Became king on **November 9, 1953**, after Abdulaziz’s death.  
- Oversaw modernization projects: schools, hospitals, and infrastructure.  
- Expanded oil revenues but struggled with financial mismanagement.  
- Faced growing tensions with his brother, Crown Prince Faisal.  

## Challenges
- Economic difficulties due to overspending.  
- Political instability within the royal family.  
- Increasing pressure from Faisal, who eventually assumed more authority.  

## End of Reign
- In **1964**, King Saud was deposed by the royal family and replaced by **King Faisal**.  
- Lived in exile in Greece until his death.  
- Died: **February 23, 1969**, in Athens, Greece.  
- Buried in **Al‑Oud Cemetery, Riyadh**.  

## Legacy
- Remembered for early modernization efforts in Saudi Arabia.  
- His reign highlighted the challenges of balancing tradition, modernization, and financial management.  
- His removal marked a turning point, leading to Faisal’s more disciplined and reformist leadership.
