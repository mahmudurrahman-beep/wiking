## Early Life
- Full name: **Eldrick Tont Woods**.  
- Nicknamed “Tiger” after a family friend from Vietnam.  
- Born in **Cypress, California**.  
- Showed prodigious talent from a young age, winning **three consecutive U.S. Junior Amateur Championships**.  
- Attended **Stanford University** before turning professional in 1996.  

## Professional Career
- Turned pro: **1996**.  
- Won his first major, the **Masters Tournament in 1997**, by a record 12 strokes.  
- Career highlights:  
  - **Masters wins:** 1997, 2001, 2002, 2005, 2019.  
  - **PGA Championships:** 1999, 2000, 2006, 2007.  
  - **U.S. Open:** 2000, 2002, 2008.  
  - **The Open Championship:** 2000, 2005, 2006.  
- Held the **world No. 1 ranking for 683 weeks**, the longest in history[43dcd9a7-70db-4a1f-b0ae-981daa162054](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tiger_Woods?citationMarker=43dcd9a7-70db-4a1f-b0ae-981daa162054&citationId=1 "Wikipedia").  

## Achievements
- **15 major championships** (second only to Jack Nicklaus with 18).  
- **82 PGA Tour wins** (tied all-time record).  
- Revolutionized golf’s popularity, bringing in new audiences and sponsorships.  
- First athlete to earn over **$1 billion in career earnings**.  

## Challenges
- Multiple injuries, including serious back and leg surgeries.  
- Personal scandals in 2009 led to a temporary decline in form.  
- Made a historic comeback by winning the **2019 Masters**, his first major in 11 years.  

## Legacy
- Considered one of the most influential athletes ever.  
- Inspired a new generation of golfers worldwide.  
- Continues to play selectively while mentoring his son, **Charlie Woods**, who is also a rising golfer.
