## Early Life
- Born: **February 12, 1809**, in LaRue County, Kentucky.  
- Grew up in poverty, largely self-educated.  
- Worked as a farmhand, rail-splitter, and later became a lawyer.  

## Political Career
- Served in the **Illinois State Legislature (1834–1842)**.  
- U.S. Congressman (1847–1849).  
- Gained national attention during debates with **Stephen A. Douglas** in 1858.  
- Elected **16th President in 1860** as a Republican.  

## Presidency
- Faced immediate crisis: **Southern secession** and outbreak of the Civil War.  
- Issued the **Emancipation Proclamation (1863)**, declaring enslaved people in Confederate states free.  
- Delivered the **Gettysburg Address (1863)**, redefining the purpose of the war as a fight for liberty and equality.  
- Re-elected in **1864**, continued to push for abolition.  

## Assassination
- On **April 14, 1865**, shot by **John Wilkes Booth** at Ford’s Theatre in Washington, D.C.  
- Died the next morning, **April 15, 1865**, at age 56.  
- His death shocked the nation, just as the Civil War was ending.  

## Legacy
- Known as the **“Great Emancipator”** and one of America’s greatest presidents.  
- Preserved the Union and ended slavery.  
- Remembered for his humility, eloquence, and leadership during crisis.
