## Introduction
**Bronze** is an alloy primarily composed of **copper** and **tin**, though other elements such as aluminum, nickel, or zinc may be added. It is one of the earliest alloys created by humans and played a crucial role in the development of civilization.

## History / Origins
- First produced around **3300 BCE**, marking the beginning of the **Bronze Age**.  
- Early civilizations in Mesopotamia, Egypt, China, and the Indus Valley used bronze for tools, weapons, and art.  
- The widespread use of bronze revolutionized technology, trade, and warfare.  
- Eventually replaced by **iron** in many applications during the Iron Age (around 1200 BCE).

## Legacy / Impact
- Defined the **Bronze Age**, a major period in human history.  
- Enabled stronger tools and weapons, advancing agriculture and warfare.  
- Still valued today for its durability and aesthetic qualities.  
- Symbolizes technological progress and cultural development in ancient societies.
