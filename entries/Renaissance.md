## Definition
The **Renaissance** was a cultural, intellectual, and artistic movement in Europe between the 14th and 17th centuries. It marked the transition from the medieval period to the early modern age, emphasizing humanism, science, and the revival of classical learning.

## Origins
- Began in **Italy (Florence, Venice, Rome)** in the 14th century.  
- Inspired by rediscovery of ancient Greek and Roman texts.  
- Fueled by wealth from trade, banking, and patronage (e.g., the Medici family).  

## Key Features
- **Humanism** – Focus on human potential, secular learning, and classical texts.  
- **Art** – Realism, perspective, and emphasis on the human form.  
- **Science** – Advances in astronomy, anatomy, and physics.  
- **Literature** – Vernacular writing, exploration of individual identity.  
- **Exploration** – Age of Discovery expanded horizons beyond Europe.  

## Major Figures
- **Leonardo da Vinci** – Artist, scientist, inventor (*Mona Lisa*, *The Last Supper*).  
- **Michelangelo Buonarroti** – Sculptor and painter (*David*, Sistine Chapel ceiling).  
- **Niccolò Machiavelli** – Political thinker (*The Prince*).  
- **Dante Alighieri** – Poet (*Divine Comedy*).  
- **Galileo Galilei** – Astronomer and physicist.  
- **William Shakespeare** – Playwright and poet.  

## Achievements
- Development of **linear perspective** in art.  
- Printing press (Johannes Gutenberg, c. 1450) revolutionized knowledge dissemination.  
- Scientific discoveries challenged medieval worldviews.  
- Flourishing of literature in vernacular languages.  
- Expansion of global trade and exploration.  

## Spread
- From Italy to France, Germany, England, Spain, and the Low Countries.  
- Northern Renaissance emphasized religious reform and printing.  

## Legacy
- Laid the foundation for the **Scientific Revolution** and **Enlightenment**.  
- Transformed art, science, politics, and education.  
- Encouraged secularism, individualism, and critical inquiry.  
- Considered one of the most influential cultural movements in human history.  

---

*The Renaissance was a rebirth of classical learning and creativity, reshaping Europe and paving the way for the modern world.*
