##Definition
**Thomas Alva Edison** was an American inventor, scientist, and businessman who held over **1,000 patents**. He is best known for his contributions to electric power, sound recording, and motion pictures, earning him the nickname *“The Wizard of Menlo Park.”*

## Early Life
- Born: **February 11, 1847**, in Milan, Ohio, USA.  
- Grew up in Port Huron, Michigan.  
- Had little formal schooling; largely self‑taught.  
- Worked as a telegraph operator, which sparked his interest in electrical technology.  

## Major Inventions
- **Phonograph (1877)** – First device to record and reproduce sound.  
- **Incandescent Light Bulb (1879)** – Improved practical electric lighting.  
- **Motion Picture Camera (Kinetoscope, 1891)** – Early moving image technology.  
- **Electric Power Distribution** – Developed systems for generating and distributing electricity.  
- Other inventions: stock ticker, alkaline storage battery, improved telegraph systems.  

## Menlo Park Laboratory
- Established in **1876** in New Jersey.  
- First industrial research lab in the world.  
- Produced many of Edison’s most famous inventions.  

## Business Ventures
- Founded **General Electric (GE)**, one of the largest companies in the world.  
- Promoted widespread electrification in cities.  
- Played a key role in the “War of Currents” against Nikola Tesla and George Westinghouse (AC vs. DC power).  

## Legacy
- Held **1,093 U.S. patents**.  
- Revolutionized modern life with electric lighting, recorded sound, and motion pictures.  
- Remembered as one of history’s greatest inventors, though also criticized for aggressive business tactics.  
- Died: **October 18, 1931**, in West Orange, New Jersey.  

---

*Thomas Edison’s inventions and entrepreneurial spirit transformed everyday life, making him a central figure in the history of technology and innovation.*
