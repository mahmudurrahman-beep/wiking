## Introduction
**Genocide** is the deliberate and systematic destruction of a national, ethnic, racial, or religious group. It is recognized as one of the gravest crimes under international law.

## History / Origins
- The term was coined in **1944** by Polish lawyer **Raphael Lemkin**, combining the Greek *genos* (race or tribe) and the Latin *-cide* (killing).  
- First legally defined in the **1948 United Nations Convention on the Prevention and Punishment of the Crime of Genocide (Genocide Convention)**.  
- The Holocaust during World War II was the defining example that led to the formal recognition of genocide as a crime.  

## Key Features
- **Definition (UN Convention)**: Acts committed with intent to destroy, in whole or in part, a group, including:  
  - Killing members of the group.  
  - Causing serious bodily or mental harm.  
  - Deliberately inflicting conditions of life calculated to bring about destruction.  
  - Imposing measures to prevent births.  
  - Forcibly transferring children.  
- **Legal Framework**:  
  - Prosecuted by international tribunals (e.g., ICTR, ICTY).  
  - Under jurisdiction of the **International Criminal Court (ICC)**.
