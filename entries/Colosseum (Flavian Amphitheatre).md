## Introduction
The **Colosseum** is an ancient amphitheatre in **Rome, Italy**, built during the Roman Empire. It is the largest amphitheatre ever constructed and one of the most iconic ruins in the world. Its round, hollow design with partially broken walls makes it instantly recognizable.

## History / Origins
- Commissioned by Emperor **Vespasian** around **70â€“72 CE**.  
- Completed in **80 CE** under Emperor **Titus**, with later modifications by Domitian.  
- Used for gladiatorial contests, public spectacles, and dramas based on classical mythology.  

## Key Features
- **Shape**: Elliptical, measuring 189 meters long and 156 meters wide.  
- **Capacity**: Could hold up to 50,000â€“80,000 spectators.  
- **Structure**: Built with concrete and stone, featuring multiple tiers of arches.  
- **Ruins**: Outer walls partially collapsed due to earthquakes and stone quarrying in the Middle Ages.  
- **Arena**: Central stage with underground chambers (hypogeum) for gladiators and animals.  

## Legacy / Impact
- Symbol of ancient Roman engineering and culture.  
- Declared a **UNESCO World Heritage Site** in 1980.  
- Attracts millions of visitors annually as one of the most famous landmarks in the world.  
- Continues to inspire modern stadium design.
