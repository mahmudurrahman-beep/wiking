## Definition
**Jesus Christ** is the central figure of Christianity, regarded by Christians as the **Son of God** and the **Messiah (Christ)** prophesied in the Hebrew Bible. He is also recognized as a prophet in Islam and revered in many world traditions.

## Early Life
- Born: Traditionally dated around **4–6 BCE** in **Bethlehem**.  
- Parents: **Mary** and **Joseph**.  
- Raised in **Nazareth**, hence often called *Jesus of Nazareth*.  
- Worked as a carpenter before beginning his ministry.  

## Ministry
- Began public ministry around age 30.  
- Preached about the **Kingdom of God**, love, forgiveness, and repentance.  
- Performed miracles: healing the sick, feeding the hungry, calming storms, raising the dead.  
- Gathered disciples, including the Twelve Apostles.  
- Delivered teachings such as the **Sermon on the Mount**.  

## Crucifixion and Resurrection
- Arrested in Jerusalem and crucified under Roman governor **Pontius Pilate** (c. 30–33 CE).  
- Christians believe he died for humanity’s sins.  
- According to Christian faith, he rose from the dead on the **third day** (Resurrection).  
- The Resurrection is celebrated as **Easter**.  

## Legacy
- His life and teachings are recorded in the **New Testament Gospels**.  
- Christianity grew from his followers, becoming the world’s largest religion.  
- Teachings emphasize love, compassion, humility, and faith.  
- Revered as a prophet in Islam, where he is called **ʿĪsā ibn Maryam**.  

## Importance
- Central to Christian theology: salvation through faith in Christ.  
- Inspired countless works of art, literature, and philosophy.  
- His message continues to shape global culture, ethics, and spirituality.  

---

*Jesus Christ is remembered as a teacher, healer, and savior whose life and message transformed human history and continues to inspire billions worldwide.*
