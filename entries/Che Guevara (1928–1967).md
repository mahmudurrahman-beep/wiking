## Early Life
- Born: **May 14, 1928**, Rosario, Argentina.  
- Studied medicine at the University of Buenos Aires.  
- His travels across Latin America exposed him to poverty and inequality, shaping his revolutionary ideology.  

## Role in the Cuban Revolution
- Joined Fidel Castro’s **26th of July Movement** in Mexico (1955).  
- Helped lead guerrilla warfare against Fulgencio Batista’s regime in Cuba.  
- After victory in **1959**, became a key figure in the new Cuban government.  

## Government Positions
- Served as **President of the National Bank of Cuba** (1959–1961).  
- Later became **Minister of Industries** (1961–1965).  
- Advocated for industrialization and socialist economic policies.  

## Global Revolutionary Efforts
- Promoted Marxist revolutions abroad.  
- Fought in the Congo (1965) and later in Bolivia (1966–1967).  
- Captured and executed by the Bolivian army on **October 9, 1967**, with CIA assistance.  

## Legacy
- Seen as a **martyr of revolutionary struggle** by supporters.  
- Criticized for harsh methods and executions during the revolution.  
- His image, especially the famous **“Guerrillero Heroico” portrait**, became a worldwide symbol of rebellion.  

---

*Che Guevara remains one of the most iconic and controversial figures of the 20th century, celebrated as a revolutionary hero by some and condemned as a violent radical by others.*
