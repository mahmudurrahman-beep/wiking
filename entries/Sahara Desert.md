# Sahara Desert

## Introduction
The **Sahara Desert** is the largest hot desert in the world, covering much of **North Africa**. It spans approximately **9.2 million square kilometers (3.6 million square miles)**, making it nearly the size of the United States or China.

## History / Origins
- The Sahara has alternated between desert and savanna over the past **several million years**, influenced by shifts in Earthâ€™s orbit and climate.  
- Ancient rock art found in the Sahara shows evidence of rivers, lakes, and abundant wildlife thousands of years ago.  
- Human civilizations, including the **Egyptians, Berbers, and Tuareg**, have long adapted to its harsh environment.

- **Wildlife**: Includes camels, fennec foxes, addax antelopes, desert reptiles, and migratory birds.  
- **People**: Nomadic groups like the **Tuareg** and **Bedouins** continue to live in the Sahara, relying on trade, herding, and oasis agriculture.
