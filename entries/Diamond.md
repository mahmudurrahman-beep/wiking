## Introduction
**Diamond** is a crystalline form of carbon, renowned as the hardest natural material known. It is prized for its brilliance, durability, and rarity, making it one of the most valuable gemstones in the world.

## History / Origins
- Known and used since antiquity, with records of diamonds in India as early as **4th century BCE**.  
- Historically traded along the Silk Road and prized by royalty.  
- Modern diamond mining began in the **19th century** in South Africa, leading to global trade.  
- Today, diamonds are mined in Africa, Russia, Canada, and Australia.  

## Key Features
- **Composition**: Pure carbon atoms arranged in a tetrahedral lattice.  
- **Hardness**: 10 on the Mohs scale â€” the hardest known natural substance.  
- **Appearance**: Transparent, brilliant, often colorless but can occur in shades (blue, pink, yellow, black).  
- **Uses**:  
  - Jewelry (rings, necklaces, crowns).  
  - Industrial applications (cutting, drilling, grinding tools).  
  - Scientific uses (semiconductors, optics, quantum research).
