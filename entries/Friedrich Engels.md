## Early Life
- Born: **28 November 1820**, Barmen, Prussia (now Wuppertal, Germany).  
- Came from a wealthy textile family, which gave him firsthand exposure to industrial capitalism.  
- Partnered with Mary Burns, an Irish working-class woman who influenced his views on labor conditions.  

## Major Works
- **The Communist Manifesto (1848):** Co‑authored with Karl Marx, calling for workers’ revolution.  
- **The Condition of the Working Class in England (1845):** Exposed harsh realities of industrial labor.  
- **Anti‑Dühring (1878):** Critique of rival socialist theories.  
- **The Origin of the Family, Private Property and the State (1884):** Analysis of social structures.  

## Collaboration with Marx
- Lifelong collaborator and supporter of Karl Marx.  
- Helped edit and publish Marx’s *Das Kapital* after Marx’s death.  
- Provided financial support to Marx, enabling his research and writing.  

## Political Activity
- Active in the **Communist League** and the **First International**.  
- Advocated for revolutionary socialism and workers’ rights.  
- His writings shaped socialist and communist movements worldwide.  

## Death & Legacy
- Died: **5 August 1895**, London, England.  
- Buried at Highgate Cemetery, near Karl Marx.  
- Remembered as the **co‑founder of Marxist theory**, blending philosophy, economics, and social critique.
