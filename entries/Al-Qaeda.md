# Al-Qaeda

# Al-Qaeda Overview

## Origins
- Founded in **1988** by **Osama bin Laden**, Abdullah Azzam, and other militants.  
- Emerged from the **Soviet–Afghan War**, where foreign fighters joined Afghan mujahideen.  
- Name means **“The Base”** in Arabic.  

## Ideology
- Salafi-jihadist movement aiming to establish a global Islamic caliphate.  
- Opposes Western influence in Muslim countries.  
- Promotes violent jihad against perceived enemies of Islam.  

## Major Attacks
- **1998:** Bombings of U.S. embassies in Kenya and Tanzania.  
- **2000:** Attack on USS Cole in Yemen.  
- **2001:** **9/11 attacks** on the World Trade Center and Pentagon, killing nearly 3,000 people.  

## Global Network
- Operates through affiliated groups worldwide:  
  - **Al-Qaeda in the Arabian Peninsula (AQAP)** – Yemen.  
  - **Al-Qaeda in the Islamic Maghreb (AQIM)** – North Africa.  
  - **Al-Shabaab** – Somalia.  
- Decentralized structure, with regional branches carrying out attacks.  

## Decline & Legacy
- Osama bin Laden killed in **2011** by U.S. forces in Pakistan.  
- Leadership passed to **Ayman al-Zawahiri** (died in 2022).  
- Still active but overshadowed by ISIS in recent years.  
- Remains a threat through affiliates and ideology.
