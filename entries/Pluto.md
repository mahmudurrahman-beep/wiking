## Introduction
Pluto is a **dwarf planet** in the Kuiper Belt, located beyond Neptune. Once considered the ninth planet of the Solar System, it was reclassified in **2006** by the International Astronomical Union (IAU). Despite its small size, Pluto remains one of the most fascinating objects in planetary science.

## History / Origins
- Discovered in **1930** by Clyde Tombaugh at the Lowell Observatory.  
- Named after the Roman god of the underworld, suggested by an 11-year-old girl, Venetia Burney.  
- Initially hailed as the ninth planet, but later discoveries of similar-sized objects led to its reclassification.
