## Geography & Status
- Coordinates: **31°46′N, 35°13′E**.  
- Situated in the Judean Hills between the Mediterranean Sea and the Dead Sea.  
- **Administered by Israel**, but also claimed by **Palestine**.  
- Divided into **East Jerusalem** (Palestinian claim, includes Old City) and **West Jerusalem** (Israeli administration).  

## Historical Timeline
- **3000–2800 BCE:** Earliest settlement near the Gihon Spring.  
- **c. 1000 BCE:** King David establishes Jerusalem as capital of Israelite kingdom.  
- **586 BCE:** Babylonian conquest, destruction of the First Temple.  
- **516 BCE:** Second Temple built, later expanded by Herod the Great.  
- **70 CE:** Romans destroy the Second Temple.  
- **7th century CE:** Islamic rule begins; Dome of the Rock and Al‑Aqsa Mosque constructed.  
- **1099–1291:** Crusades bring Christian control intermittently.  
- **1517–1917:** Ottoman Empire rule.  
- **1917–1948:** British Mandate period.  
- **1948:** Division after Arab–Israeli War.  
- **1967:** Israel captures East Jerusalem in Six‑Day War.  
- **1980:** Israel declares Jerusalem its capital; not internationally recognized.  

## Religious Significance
- **Judaism:** Site of the First and Second Temples; Western Wall remains a sacred site.  
- **Christianity:** Location of the **Church of the Holy Sepulchre**, believed to be where Jesus was crucified and resurrected.  
- **Islam:** Home to the **Al‑Aqsa Mosque** and **Dome of the Rock**, third holiest sites in Islam.  

## Modern Context
- Population: Over **900,000**.  
- A center of **Israeli–Palestinian conflict**, with disputes over sovereignty.  
- Important landmarks: **Temple Mount/Haram al‑Sharif**, **Western Wall**, **Church of the Holy Sepulchre**, **Mount of Olives**.
