# NATO (North Atlantic Treaty Organization)

## Definition
The **North Atlantic Treaty Organization (NATO)** is a political and military alliance that guarantees collective defense: an attack against one member is considered an attack against all (Article 5 of the NATO Treaty).

## Origins
- Founded: **April 4, 1949** in Washington, D.C.  
- Purpose: To counter Soviet influence after World War II and maintain peace in Europe.  
- Founding members: 12 countries including the U.S., Canada, U.K., France, Italy, and others.  

## Current Membership
- **32 member states** (as of 2024).  
- Latest members: **Finland (2023)** and **Sweden (2024)**.  
- Headquarters: **Brussels, Belgium**.  
- Official languages: **English and French**.  

## Principles
- **Collective Defense** – Article 5 ensures mutual protection.  
- **Democracy & Cooperation** – Members commit to democratic values.  
- **Deterrence & Security** – Maintains military readiness to deter threats.  

## Leadership
- Current Secretary‑General: **Mark Rutte** (since 2024).  
- Military structure led by the **NATO Military Committee** and Supreme Allied Commanders.  

## Importance
- Provides stability and security in Europe and North America.  
- Coordinates military operations, peacekeeping, and disaster relief.  
- Plays a central role in responding to modern challenges like cyber threats and terrorism.  

---

*From its Cold War origins to today’s global security challenges, NATO remains a cornerstone of international defense and cooperation.*
