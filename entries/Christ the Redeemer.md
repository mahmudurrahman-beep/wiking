## Introduction
**Christ the Redeemer** (*Cristo Redentor*) is a colossal statue of **Jesus Christ** located in **Rio de Janeiro, Brazil**. It stands atop the **Corcovado Mountain** in the Tijuca Forest National Park, overlooking the city. It is one of the most iconic landmarks in Latin America and a symbol of Christianity worldwide.

## History / Origins
- Conceived in the early 20th century to celebrate Brazilâ€™s centennial of independence.  
- Designed by Brazilian engineer **Heitor da Silva Costa**, with French sculptor **Paul Landowski** creating the statue.  
- Construction began in **1922** and was completed in **1931**.  
- Funded largely through donations from Brazilian Catholics.

## Legacy / Impact
- Declared one of the **New Seven Wonders of the World** in 2007.  
- Serves as a cultural and religious symbol of Brazil.  
- Attracts millions of visitors annually, making it one of the most photographed monuments in the world.
