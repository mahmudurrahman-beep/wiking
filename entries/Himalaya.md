## Geography
- Length: ~2,400 km (1,500 miles).  
- Width: 200–400 km.  
- Countries: India, Nepal, Bhutan, Pakistan, China.  
- Highest peak: **Mount Everest (8,848.86 m)**.  
- Other major peaks: Kangchenjunga (8,586 m), Lhotse (8,516 m), Makalu (8,462 m), Cho Oyu (8,201 m).  

## Formation
- Formed by the **collision of the Indian Plate and Eurasian Plate** about 50 million years ago.  
- Still rising due to ongoing tectonic activity.  
- Part of the **Alpine orogeny**.  

## Climate & Environment
- Acts as a barrier to cold winds from Central Asia, shaping South Asia’s monsoon climate.  
- Contains vast glaciers and rivers (Indus, Ganges, Brahmaputra).  
- Home to diverse ecosystems: alpine meadows, subtropical forests, and high-altitude deserts.  

## Cultural & Religious Significance
- Sacred in **Hinduism, Buddhism, and Jainism**.  
- Pilgrimage sites: Mount Kailash, Amarnath, Kedarnath, and others.  
- Symbol of spiritual retreat and resilience.  

## Modern Importance
- Source of water for nearly **1.5 billion people**.  
- Popular for trekking, mountaineering, and tourism.  
- Faces challenges: **climate change, glacial melt, earthquakes, and deforestation**.
