Aristotle (384–322 BCE) was a Greek philosopher and polymath, student of Plato, and tutor to Alexander the Great. He made foundational contributions to logic, science, ethics, and politics.

## Life and Background
- Born in Stagira, Macedonia.  
- Studied at Plato’s Academy for 20 years.  
- Founded the Lyceum in Athens.  

## Philosophy
1. **Logic** – Developed syllogism and formal reasoning.  
2. **Ethics** – Advocated the "Golden Mean" between extremes.  
3. **Politics** – Classified governments and emphasized civic virtue.  
4. **Science** – Observed biology, physics, and natural phenomena systematically.  

## Works
- *Nicomachean Ethics*  
- *Politics*  
- *Metaphysics*  
- *Poetics*
