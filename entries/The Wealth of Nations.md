## Introduction
*An Inquiry into the Nature and Causes of the Wealth of Nations* (commonly known as *The Wealth of Nations*) is a book published in **1776** by Scottish economist and philosopher **Adam Smith**. It is considered the foundation of modern economics and a key text in classical liberal thought.

## History / Origins
- Published on **9 March 1776**, the same year as the American Declaration of Independence.  
- Written during the Scottish Enlightenment, a period of intellectual and cultural flourishing.  
- Adam Smith sought to explain how nations build wealth and how economic systems function.  

## Key Features
- **Division of Labor**: Productivity increases when tasks are specialized.  
- **Invisible Hand**: Markets self-regulate through individuals pursuing their own interests.  
- **Free Markets**: Advocated minimal government interference in trade and commerce.  
- **Value and Price**: Distinguished between ‚Äúreal value‚Äù (labor) and ‚Äúmarket price.‚Äù  
- **Taxes and Public Goods**: Supported limited taxation to fund defense, justice, and infrastructure.  

## Legacy / Impact
- Established economics as a distinct discipline.  
- Influenced policies promoting free trade and capitalism.  
- Continues to shape debates on markets, government intervention, and economic growth.  
- Considered one of the most influential books ever written in social science.
